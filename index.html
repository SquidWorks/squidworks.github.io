Welcome to Squid Works

This is the soon to be home of the best training framework for cyber security on the internet. Shoutout to Github for hosting it and Slack for approving it.

Everyone wants to learn how to cyber, but very quickly, it gets hard. Insurmountable really. We don't like that. So I made this site.

Training that make sense.

  • No expectation of technical knowledge
  • Starts with general overview of technical topics and theory
  • Goes over the different types of things you can learn
  • Provides a baseline knowledge and explains why those fundamentals are essential
  • Provides a robust curriculum for when someone is done with the introductory course
  • Community support

Aggregating useful resources is nice and all (looking at you pastebin aggregations) but that makes learning security very daunting. Do you start with buffer overflows, or traffic analysis, or some forensics? How about vocabulary and the fundamentals? Here, we start off with the basics and introduce the fundamental concepts required for learning.



Our goal isn't to teach you how to hack,

our goal is to teach you how to learn.

Training

This site is based off of a totally not peer reviewed study on the lack of useful materials to bring the absolute beginner up to speed on cybersecurity issues. I taught myself and tried to teach people for three years and identified a ton of problems, so I designed this site to address them. Hopefully I'll get around to actually doing a formal study on this.
